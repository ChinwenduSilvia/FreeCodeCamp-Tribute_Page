Life of Maya Angelou

Timeline

  • 1928

    An American poet is born

    Marguerite Annie Johnson was born in St. Louis, Missuori on April 4, 1928. She is the second child born to Bailey and Vivian (Baxter) Johnson).

  • 1931

    Child of a broken home

    Her parents ended their calamitous marriage, and she and her brother were sent to live with their paternal grandmother

  • 1936

    Raped by mother's boyfriend

    She returned back to her mother at St. Louis and was sexually abused by her mother's boyfriend, a man named Freeman.

  • 1944

    First black female cable car conductor

    Her mother referred to it as her "dream job" and warned her to always arrive earl and work harder than others

  • 1945

    Guy Johnson is born

    Three weeks after completing high school, at the age of 17, she gave birth to her son, Clyde now known as Guy Johnson.

  • 1951

    Wedding bells

    She married Tosh Angelos, a Greek electrician, former sailor and aspiring musician despite her mother's disapproval

  • 1954

    The lonely road of divorce

    Angelou's marriage ended, and began dancing professionally in clubs in the USA and Europe

  • 1959

    Writing career

    Novelist John Oliver Killens urged Angelou to concentrate on her writing career, thereafter she joined the Harlem Writers Guild.

  • 1960

    Anti-Aparthied Activism

    organized "the legendary" Cabaret for Freedom to benefit the Southern Christian Leadership Conference (SCLC), after meeting with Martin Luther King.

  • 1962

    Life in Ghana

    She became the administrator at the University of Ghana, She was a feature editor for The African Review, a freelance writer for the Ghanaian Times, wrote and broadcast for Radio Ghana.

  • 1972

    Georgia

    Angelou's Georgia, Georgia, produced by a Swedish film company and filmed in Sweden, the first screenplay written by a black woman.

  • 1981

    Professor Maya

    Despite havng no bachelor's degree, accepted the lifetime Reynolds Professorship of American Studies at Wake Forest University in Winston-Salem, North Carolina, where she was one of a few full-time African-American professors.

  • 1993

    Inaugural Recitation

    Angelou recited her poem "On the Pulse of Morning" at the presidential inauguration of Bill Clinton, becoming the first poet to make an inaugural recitation.

  • 1996

    Directs a feature film

    Angelou achieved her goal of directing a feature film in 1996, Down in the Delta, which featured actors such as Alfre Woodard and Wesley Snipes.

  • 2008

    Presidential primaries campaign

    Angelou campaigned for the Democratic Party in the 2008 presidential primaries, giving her public support to Hillary Clinton.

  • 2011

    Presidential Medal of Freedom

    Former US President Barack Obama presenting Angelou with the Presidential Medal of Freedom.

  • 2014

    A legend passes away

    Angelou died on the morning of May 28, 2014
